The design language

Clarity is the craft.

Running your own site is real work that deserves a clear desk. The admin grew into four dashboards bolted edge to edge — loud, mismatched, squinting-small. Quire is the argument that it can be one quiet, legible place.

What the name means

A quire is a gathering of loose sheets bound into one section of a book. That is the whole mission in one word: WordPress's admin today is loose, mismatched pages from four different printers — wp-admin, Gutenberg, WooCommerce, Jetpack — never bound together. Quire gathers the scattered pages into one coherent whole.

Binding is not decorating. We don't dress the admin up; we take things away until what remains is only what informs or acts. One typeface. Two backgrounds. Black ink for action, gray for everything quiet, and colour only when something genuinely has a status. Every screen answers the same three questions the same way: where am I, what matters, what can I do.

Legible to everyone, or it isn't design

Accessibility is not our checklist; it is our aesthetic. Body text holds AAA contrast, states are unmistakable without relying on colour, one honest focus ring follows the keyboard everywhere, and the type is big enough to read without leaning in. A screen a tired person can read at a glance and a screen that looks calm are the same screen — we refuse to treat those as separate goals.

This is also why the palette is strict. When colour appears only as meaning — green is success, amber is warning, red is danger, blue is information, and nothing else — colour becomes a language instead of a mood. The quieter the page, the louder the one thing that matters.

Restraint is a position, not a mood

Nothing here decorates. No marker bars, no uppercase letterspaced labels, no filled chips, no gradients. Hierarchy comes from size and space; state comes from tone and weight; separation comes from a 1px hairline, and shadow exists only for things that truly float. When an element neither informs nor acts, it goes.

We're building an admin meant to age in an industry that worships whatever shipped this morning. Black, white, gray, and one good typeface don't go out of style — restraint is what still feels right in ten years. The calm isn't decoration. It's the position.

For the person at the keyboard

And it's for someone specific: a writer, a shopkeeper, a teacher, a volunteer — not an engineer. Today's admin makes them feel they're operating machinery they don't understand. Quire's job is to return the feeling the act deserves: dignity. You are not configuring a system. You are tending your own publication.

The place where you run your site should be calm, clear, and legible to everyone — one quiet desk that's yours — not a control panel you operate.
What this commits us to
  • Coherence is respect.
    Every clashing button is a small tax on someone's attention and confidence. Making everything mean one thing is how we respect the person using it.
  • Calm is the feature.
    The work is subtraction — fewer colours, fewer styles, less noise. A quiet screen is a finished screen. We never add a control to solve a problem we could remove.
  • Legible to everyone, or it isn't design.
    AAA body text, unmistakable states, one honest focus ring. If someone can't read it, nothing else about it matters.
  • Built to age, not to trend.
    Black, white, gray, and one typeface don't go out of style. Restraint is what keeps a system right in ten years, not novel for one.
  • A commons, not a product.
    Open, GPL, owned by the community that runs a large share of the web. Quire answers to no vendor.
Typography — one typeface, many weights

Type carries most of the feeling, so it gets the strictest rule of all: one family. Inter — built for interfaces, exceptionally legible small, dignified large — set at a generous scale and carried entirely by weight and tone. No serif for ceremony, no mono for costume; when everything speaks in one voice, hierarchy comes from what is said, not what it's dressed in.

Where we're going

Today Quire is a coherent skin over the existing admin. The longer arc is a genuine reimagining of what running a WordPress site feels like — but at every stage the immutable core is the feeling: one quiet, legible desk, not a machine room.

The detailed rules and the locked grammar live in DESIGN.md. This page is its front door — the why, before the what.
